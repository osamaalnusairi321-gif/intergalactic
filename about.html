Company

A small team with a planetary problem.

Ntergalactic began with a simple observation: enormous amounts of organic waste are discarded every day, while demand for cleaner and more distributed energy continues to grow.

Our story

Turn waste into watts.

Founded by University of Oklahoma students Ahmed Alnusairi and Anayat Yousuf, Ntergalactic combines electrical engineering, mathematics, biology, and public health perspectives.

The idea grew from firsthand exposure to food waste in university food service. Instead of accepting waste as a disposal problem, the founders began exploring microbial fuel cells as a way to recover electrical energy from biodegradable material.

The company is developing both physical systems and software tools that can make experimentation, optimization, and future field deployment more practical.

What guides us

Build rigorously. Communicate honestly. Think long-term.

01 — Practicality

Research must move toward use.

We focus on tools and systems that can eventually support real experimentation and field deployment.

02 — Transparency

Do not overclaim the science.

Early-stage technology earns trust through evidence, careful language, and measurable progress.

03 — Scale

Design for distributed impact.

Our long-term interest is energy generation close to the waste streams that create the opportunity.
