# Ntergalactic website audit and redesign direction

## What the existing Wix site gets right

- The core mission is memorable: converting organic waste into clean electricity.
- “Turn Wastes into Watts” is a strong seed for a brand platform.
- The company has a real product, MFCOptimizer Pilot 1.0, rather than only an abstract vision.
- The founder story connects engineering, biology, public health, and firsthand exposure to food waste.
- BioBatteryLab gives the company a broader initiative beyond a single software product.

## What currently weakens the site

### 1. The hero image tells the wrong story
The homepage opens with a consumer drone, which has no clear relationship to microbial fuel cells, organic waste, or Ntergalactic’s product. It creates immediate confusion.

### 2. The website looks like an unfinished platform template
The menu exposes generic Wix pages such as Groups, Members, and Search Results. Placeholder contact details also appear in parts of the site. These signals reduce trust.

### 3. The message lacks hierarchy
Products, partners, projects, vision, and contact content compete for attention. The visitor is not guided through one clear story.

### 4. The product is presented like a generic store item
MFCOptimizer needs product storytelling: who it is for, what problem it solves, how it works, what it does not claim to do, and why it matters. A basic price card is not enough.

### 5. The science is not visualized
The strongest opportunity is to explain the process simply: organic input → microbial activity → electron capture → electrical output. The old site describes this but does not make it visually intuitive.

### 6. Credibility is underdeveloped
The founders are shown, but the site needs clearer evidence over time: prototypes, lab images, measured results, university work, pilot milestones, research references, partnerships, and product screenshots.

### 7. Some language is too expansive for an early-stage company
Claims involving global scale or civilization-level energy control can make a serious technical audience skeptical. The redesign keeps the ambition but uses more disciplined language.

## New design strategy

### Positioning
Ntergalactic is an early-stage microbial energy company building physical systems and intelligent software tools that help explore how organic waste can produce electricity.

### Primary story
**Waste into watts.**

### Visitor sequence
1. Understand the company in one sentence.
2. See the waste-to-electricity process.
3. Explore the MFCOptimizer product.
4. Understand BioBatteryLab and the long-term direction.
5. Meet the founders.
6. Contact the company for purchase, research, or collaboration.

### Visual principles
- Large editorial typography.
- Strong contrast and generous whitespace.
- One idea per section.
- Restrained organic-green accent.
- Technical diagrams rather than irrelevant stock photography.
- Motion used to explain electron flow, not as decoration.
- Premium product framing without copying another brand’s website.

## Missing material that would improve version two

- High-resolution photos of the founders.
- Photos or video of actual MFC experiments and prototypes.
- A concise product demonstration video.
- Confirmed product license terms and refund/support policy.
- Hosted checkout link.
- Real partner names and logos, only with permission.
- Measured milestones or validated results that can be stated publicly.
- Confirmed LinkedIn and social profile URLs.
- Privacy policy and terms for product sales.
